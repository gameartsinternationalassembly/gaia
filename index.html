Toronto Games Week

Toronto Games Week (TGW) is a new celebration of videogame arts and culture in the city.

The first TGW will be June 1-7th 2023 and is a collective coordination of events organized independently by dozens of organizations, creators, curators, and companies.

  • Arcade games around the city!
  • Party by Dirty Rectangles!
  • Showcase by NextGen artists!
  • Panel on new labour models in games!
  • Playtesting barbeque!
  • Workshops on refining your game hook!
  • Think tank on Indigenous game creation!
  • Talk on ancient artifacts of Nintendo!

Preservation Through Play: Different ways to save video game history and context

📍TPL (Fort York branch)

Collector and preservationist Derek Quenneville will give a talk on various approaches to keeping video game history alive, including: 3D scanning, cultural context, and hands-on play.
Attendees will get to try ancient artifacts of Nintendo, including the Game Boy Sewing Machine (2000), Game Boy Camera and Printer (1998), the Family BASIC keyboard, (1984), and the Nintendo Virtual Boy game system (1995). Hosted by Derek Quenneville.

Who's organizing Toronto Games Week?

Each event is independently organized by your favourite local game enthusiasts.

Toronto Games Week is coordinated by Marie LeBlanc Flanagan and Jim Munroe (Game Arts International Network executive director and co-founder of the Hand Eye Society).

TGW came to life with our original group of advisors, a supergroup of game community organizations, including representatives from Toronto Game Jam, Interactive Ontario, Buffer Festival, Vector Festival, DMG, Dirty Rectangles, Hand Eye Society, and Funland Magazine. Additional input was given by representatives of the Ontario Arts Council, OCAD, TIFF, and the City of Toronto IDM Office.

Code of Conduct and Covid Policy

We want people to come to TGW and participate and thrive no matter their experience, gender identity and expression, disability sexual orientation, personal appearance, race, ethnicity, body size, age, nationality, or religion. Our code of conduct is here.

Toronto Games Week strongly recommends that attendees bring and wear masks. We will have some masks at the door of all events. Each TGW event will have their own rules around COVID 19, and that information will be clearly communicated in advance on this website so people can make informed choices.

We welcome donations of masks, tests, or loans of air filters to make events safer for everyone.
